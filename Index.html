Artificial Intelligence (AI) Is Moving Fast – What Are the Ethical Implications for Scientific Research?

https://blog.primr.org/artificial-intelligence-ai-is-moving-fast-what-are-the-ethical-implications-for-scientific-research/?gad_source=1&gclid=CjwKCAjw-JG5BhBZEiwAt7JR6_ZoDLD9BCGCNVM3YSSN8QcnAJ1lr3S35vQSL8JPTXjCi0L-VYejYBoCuwIQAvD_BwE August 1,2023

The article highlights the widespread impact of AI on society, particularly in medicine and scientific research. As AI becomes integral in data-driven decision-making, researchers and ethicists are examining its ethical implications. Notable figures like Elisa Hurley and Jon Herington discuss the importance of "data ethics," emphasizing fair, representative data and avoiding exploitation in research, especially when it involves diverse populations. The growing reliance on AI brings ethical dilemmas, such as privacy concerns and "blurring lines" between personal data and research data. AI’s reliance on vast amounts of de-identified data, often managed by private companies, raises questions about accountability and oversight, as much of this data lacks regulation. PRIM&R, a research ethics organization, leads discussions on responsible AI, hosting workshops funded by NSF to explore ethical frameworks and address the risks AI poses to research ethics. Participants at these events, including experts from various institutions, tackle challenges like AI in clinical decision support, violence prevention, and the ethical involvement of diverse populations. With public concern on the rise, the article underscores the need for ethical AI practices, the role of IRBs, and a proactive approach to ensure that AI-driven research respects human rights and remains transparent. PRIM&R aims to uphold ethical standards in AI's evolving role in research.

Touchless Tech for Work: Adapting to the Latest Tech Trends

https://infiniticube.com/blog/touchless-tech-for-work-adapting-to-the-latest-tech-trends/ June 04,2024

The article explains how touchless technology—using sensors, cameras, and AI—makes workplaces safer and more efficient by removing the need for physical contact. Starting with simple motion sensors in the 1960s, touchless tech has evolved to include voice and gesture controls, which are now common in places like hospitals, stores, and offices. Main Benefits Touchless tech improves hygiene, saves time, and boosts productivity. For example, in healthcare, it reduces infection risks, while in retail, it makes shopping quicker and easier. Many businesses have seen big improvements by using touchless tech, like faster service and happier customers. Challenges Switching to touchless tech can be costly and needs careful setup and training. It also comes with technical issues, like accuracy problems, and requires staff to adapt to new ways of working. Future Trends Touchless tech is expected to keep growing, with AI, AR/VR, and smart devices making workplaces even more advanced. For companies to stay competitive, they need to be open to new tech, train their staff, and focus on security. Conclusion Touchless technology helps make workplaces cleaner, safer, and more efficient. While it can be challenging to set up, it’s worth it for companies that want to keep up with modern tech.

Unlocking the future of customer service: AI-powered virtual agents and the science of intent

https://www-2.rotman.utoronto.ca/insightshub/ai-analytics-big-data/customer-service-expert-descri Jan,2020

This article discusses how AI-powered virtual agents are transforming customer service by predicting customer intent and providing fast, efficient responses. Virtual agents use data to anticipate customer needs, enabling seamless interactions across multiple platforms like chat, phone, and smart devices. For example, United Airlines uses AI to recognize flight day calls and assist with changes, creating a smoother experience. While virtual agents can make customer service more scalable, companies must thoughtfully implement AI to address specific needs and avoid misuse, like phishing or data breaches. Human agents remain crucial for empathetic interactions, and businesses should balance AI tools with security measures. To start, organizations should assess their existing systems, identify pain points, and tailor AI solutions to enhance customer satisfaction.
